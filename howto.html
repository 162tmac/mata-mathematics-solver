How to Use MATA - Mathematics Solver

1. Entering a question to the solver.

  • Using the searchbar, enter the math problem you want to solve. You can use the dropdown or keyboard to help you with the input, then submit to solve.

  • Math symbols don't appear in the searchbar so you must enter the code for them or just use words if you're unsure. For example, to multiply you would use the '*' key and to find the square root of 2 enter 'sqrt(2)'.

  • The interative keyboard can be used to input some of these symbols. However, if you unsure you can just try words. For example, 'three multiplied by two' gets the same result as '3*2'.

2. Using the dropdown

  • Choose the math question you have that you want to solve and then input your specific problem.

  • You do not have to choose something from the dropdown to use the solver and can simply write your own problem in the searchbar.

3. Your step by step results

  • After inputting your question and submitting, your results will appear under the keyboard.

  • You can reveal how to do the full question by clicking the reveal all button or see individual steps to the solution.

  • If you are unsure how to start a math problem, we recommend revealing Step 1 and then trying to solve the question yourself from there rather that just revealing the entire answer without trying.

4. Refining your search

  • Sometimes the solver won't output the full steps or what you were expecting. You may need to go back and give it extra commands.

  • For example, if you input '2x^2 + 3x + 1', you've just inputted an expression and there is no step by step solution needed. You might add 'Factorise 2x^2 + 3x + 1' or 'Find the roots of 2x^2 + 3x + 1'.

5. Using the side calculator

  • Along with the solver, there is a side calculator that can be used to help your study. It is just a simple calculator that quickly computes smaller calculations.

  • Click on the sidebar to open, and then click anywhere on the solver page to close.
